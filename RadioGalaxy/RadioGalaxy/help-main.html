The RadioGalaxy player lets you search for and play Internet Radio stations. Once a station is selected, it begins streaming, and by default keeps streaming even if you switch to another station. When you return to the previous station, playback resumes from the point where you left off.

If you want streaming to stop when you switch away from a station, you can either press the Stop icon (the black box), or go to the More.../Settings menu and turn off 'Stream even after switch.'

Basic Operation

There are four control buttons at the bottom of the screen. The "-20" button moves back 20 seconds in the playing stream, and "+20" moves forward 20 seconds. The Play/Pause button pauses or resumes the current station, although the station keeps streaming even when playing is paused. The Stop (square) button stops both the player and the stream.

Pressing a station's icon will play or resume the station. The icon colors and animation tells you the current state:

  • Purple+Flashing
    • Currently playing radio station and streaming data.
  • Orange
    • Last playing radio station, but when the station is no longer playing or streaming data. Pressing the 'play' button will resume streaming and playing audio.
  • Blue+Flashing
    • Not playing, but still streaming audio in the background.
  • Green
    • Idle station: neither playing nor streaming data.

A long press on any station's icon will display details about the station, including its name, streaming URL and current transfer rate. The details page also contains an "Edit Station" button that let's you change any of the station parameters, or to remove the station entirely.

The Highlight button on the main screen tells RadioGalaxy to highlight the currently playing song in the history list so you can later review music you found interesting.

Adding and Editing Station Details

The first menu item in the Main Menu (the green button on the screen) is "+Station." If pressed, you can search for stations by name or keywords. The search page has its own help pages that describe in detail how to search for and add radio stations, but you can search by name, keyword, country code, city name and/or genre. If you swipe left on a validated station, you can listen to a sample of the station's stream.

If you long press a station's icon and then press the "Edit Station" button, you can edit the station's parameters, including its name, description and the station's stream URL There are also buttons to cancel the update, end the editing session and to remove the station from RadioGalaxy entirely.

Streaming Control

The Settings page has a button that enables or disables streaming in the background. If enabled (the default) the station will keep streaming data after you switch away to listen to another station, and will resume playing where you left off when you return to the original station.

If this is disabled, the stream will stop if you switch to play another station, and will resume from the latest point in the stream when selected again. However, you can still use the "-20" button or slider to play already streamed data from the station.

You can set this behavior separately for switching from the app's main screen, or when switching from the lock screen or CarPlay controls.

Menu Operations

If you press the "More..." menu button under the station icons, it pops up a list of functions RadioGalaxy can perform.

  • Manually add station
    • If a station is missing from all of our databases, you can still add a station in manually as long as you know its stream URL.
  • Help
    • Displays this text.
  • Show history
    • RadioGalaxy tracks each song played on a station, and logs it in a history file. You can highlight songs to remember by swiping on the entry. When you erase all the songs in the history, all the highlighted songs are preserved untouched, although you can manually remove highlighted songs by removing the highlight or delete them on a song by song basis.
