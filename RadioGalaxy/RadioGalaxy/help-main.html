The RadioGalaxy player lets you search for and play Internet Radio stations. Once a station is selected, it begins streaming, and by default keeps streaming even if you switch to another station. When you return to the previous station, playback resumes from where you left off.

If you want streaming to stop when you switch away from a station, you can either press the Stop icon (the black box), or press the station's icon to bring up station options, and press the "Stream only while playing" button.

Basic Operation

There are four control buttons at the bottom of the screen. The "-20" button moves back 20 seconds in the playing stream, and "+20" moves forward 20 seconds. The Play/Pause button pauses or resumes the current station, but the station keeps streaming while the playing is paused. The Stop (square shaped) button stops the player and stops the stream as well.

Pressing a station's icon will play or resume the station. If the trim around a station's icon is orange, it is the currently selected station, otherwise it is green. If the icon is flashing, it means that the station is streaming music in the background, even if it isn't the currently selected station.

Pressing the station's icon while it is already playing will bring up the station details, including the station's full name, the stream's currently transfer rate and type of stream (AAC or MP3), and the station's stream URL.

The details page also has a button that toggles betwen "Stream always" or "Stream only while playing." If you press "Stream always" (the default) the station will keep streaming data while you're listening to another station and will resume playing where you left off when you return to it. If you press "Stream only while playing" the stream will stop when you switch to play another station, and will resume from the latest point in the stream.

Another button on the details page tells RadioGalaxy to highlight the currently playing song in the history list, so you can later review music you found interesting.

Adding and Editing Station Details

The first menu item in the Main Menu (the green button on the screen) is "Search for Station." If pressed, you can search for stations by name or properties. The search page has its own help pages that describe in detail how to search for and add radio stations, but you can search by name, tag, country code, city and/or genre.

If you long press a station's icon, you can edit the station's parameters, including its name, description and the station's stream URL There are also buttons to cancel the update, end the editing session and to remove the station from RadioGalaxy entirely.

Menu Operations

If you press the green menu bar under the station icons, it pops up a list of functions RadioGalaxy can perform.

  • Search for stations
    • Selecting this operation brings up a page you can use to search our directory providers for a station. The Search page has its own more detailed help page on how to add a new station.
  • Manually add station
    • If a station is missing from all of our databases, you can still add a station in manually as long as you know its stream URL.
  • Help
    • Displays this text.
  • Show history
    • RadioGalaxy tracks each song played on a station, and logs it in a history file. You can highlight songs to remember by swiping on the entry. When you erase all the songs in the history, all the highlighted songs are preserved untouched.
