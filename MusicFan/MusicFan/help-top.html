Using AudioGalaxy

[More detailed help is available by pressing the "Menu" button at the top of the screen and the selecting "Help pages" from the menu.]

AudioGalaxy allows you to program up to 4 channels and switch between them by pressing the gold buttons at the bottom of the screen. Each channel remembers your position when you switch from it.

Press the Menu button to get started. Then press the "Define Music Channel" button to define a music channel, the "Define Podcast Channel" button to define a channel of podcasts, or "Define Radio Channel" to define a channel containing Internet Radio stations. Music channels can contain music from your iTunes cloud library, and/or music from a UPNP/DLNA server.

Once defined, you can edit a channel definition by pressing the "Channel Details" button to edit or reorder the individual song list, and from the "Channel Details" screen, you can press the "Define" button to change the actual definition of the channel.

To add music from a UPNP/DLNA server, you press "Setup UPnP Servers" from the Menu button. Then press "Find Servers" to browse for music servers; you may to "Find Servers" two or three times to find all present on your network. Then select a server and press "Load From Selected", which loads the song listings from the selected UPnP server. Once UPnP music has been loaded, it can be added to a channel through the usual "Define" button.

The "Channel Details" screen lets you reorder, download or remove items; swipe items left to see options.

Podcast items need to be downloaded before they can be played. From the Edit screen, swipe left on the episode to see load/unload options, or to see show details.

More detailed help is available by pressing the "Menu" button at the top of the screen and the selecting "Help pages" from the menu.
