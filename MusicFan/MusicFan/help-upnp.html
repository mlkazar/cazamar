Configuring UPNP / DLNA Servers

AudioGalaxy can stream music from your local DLNA / UPNP servers.

To add music from a UPNP/DLNA server, press "Find Servers" to browse for music servers (you may have to browse multiple times to find all servers on your LAN, since UPNP uses the unreliable multicast UDP protocol for device discovery).

You likely need to enable the UPNP (sometimes called DLNA) service on your Router or NAS appliance before browsing for servers will work.

Once server discovery is complete, decide which servers you want to load music from, and then select each server one at a time, and press "Load From Selected" to read the music directories and cover art from that server. Once the music directories have been loaded, music from the servers can be added through the usual Sources button.

You can redo the "Find Servers" operation to find new servers at any time, and you can redo the "Load From Selected" operation to discover newly added music on the server.

You can swipe left on the list of UPNP devices, and remove any servers, as well as the music loaded from those servers.

Once a music directory has been loaded, you do not have to reload it except to pick up new music from the server.

When you have defined a channel including music from a UPNP server, you can swipe left on individual songs in the channel list to download those songs for offline listening. You can also press "Load All" on the "Song List" screen to download all UPNP-resident music in the channel. If a song isn't downloaded and your NAS system is not available on your current network, you won't be able to play that song (surprise).
