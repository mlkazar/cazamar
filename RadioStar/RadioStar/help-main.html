The RadioStar player lets you search for and play Internet Radio stations. When playing a station, RadioStar remembers the last 120 minutes (by default) played on that station, and you can use the back ("-20") and forward ("+20") buttons to navigate within the already streamed music by adding or subtracting 20 seconds from the current playing location, or you can use the slider instead.

By default, the downloading of a station stops when you select a different station to play, and resumes when you switch back. However, if you select "Stream in background" option from the player options while playing a station, RadioStar will continue to stream music while you are playing other stations. When you switch between stations, RadioStar remembers where in the stream you were listening, and resumes from that point when you switch back to that station.

Basic Operation

There are four control buttons at the bottom of the screen. The "-20" button moves back 20 seconds in the downloaded stream, and "+20" moves forward 20 seconds. The Play/Pause button pauses or resumes the current station, but the station keeps downloading while the playing is paused. The Stop (square shaped) button stops the player and stops the download as well.

Pressing a station's icon will play or resume the station. If the trim around a station's icon is orange, it is the currently selected station, otherwise it is green. If the icon is flashing, it means that the station is currently downloading music, even if it isn't the currently selected station.

Pressing the station's icon while it is already playing will bring up the station details, including the station's full name, the stream's currently transfer rate and type of stream (AAC or MP3), and the station's URL. The navigator also has a button labeled "Stream in background", which, if pressed, sets the station to continue downloading even when another station is active. Another navigator button tells RadioStar to highlight the currently playing song in the history list, so you can later review music you found interesting.

Adding and Editing Station Details

The first menu item in the Main Menu (the green button on the screen) is "Search for Station." If pressed, you can search for stations by name or properties. The search page has its own help pages that describe in detail how to search for and add radio stations, but you can search by name, tag, country code, city and/or genre.

If you long press a station's icon, you can edit the station's parameters, including its name, description and the station's stream URL There are also buttons to cancel the update, end the editing session and a button to remove the station from RadioStar entirely.

Menu Operations

If you press the green menu bar under the station icons, it pops up a list of functions RadioStar can perform.

  • Search for stations
    • Selecting this operation brings up a page you can use to search our directory providers for a station. The Search page has its own more detailed help page on how to add a new station.
  • Manually add station
    • If a station is missing from all of our databases, you can still add a station in manually as long as you know its stream URL.
  • Help
    • Displays this text.
  • Show history
    • RadioStar tracks each song played on a station, and logs it in a history file. You can highlight songs to remember by swiping on the entry. When you erase all the songs in the history, all the highlighted songs are preserved untouched.
